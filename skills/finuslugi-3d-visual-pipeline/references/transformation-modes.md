# Transformation Modes

Choose exactly one primary mode before building the Scene Specification.

## `style-transfer`

Preserve subject, action, and major composition. Replace materials, color, light, and rendering language with the selected Finuslugi style pack.

## `reinterpretation`

Preserve the communication idea and primary metaphor. Permit a new composition, environment, and object treatment.

## `composition-transfer`

Preserve the placement, scale, and hierarchy of major masses. Replace subject matter and style.

## `object-preservation`

Preserve the exact main object or product form. Permit a new background, light, supporting elements, and style treatment.

## `brand-adaptation`

Preserve the source meaning. Rebuild the image as a new Finuslugi brand asset under one approved style pack.

## `variation`

Preserve the general scene and style. Change local details, camera, supporting objects, or pose without changing the message.

## `reference-synthesis`

Combine multiple references with non-overlapping roles into one new scene. One source may own style, another geometry, another composition, and another identity. Conflicting ownership of the same role must be resolved before generation.

## `local-correction`

Edit an existing generated image. Preserve all approved regions and change one diagnosed scene layer only.

## Default

Use `brand-adaptation` when the user asks to turn an external reference into a new Finuslugi visual and does not specify another mode.
