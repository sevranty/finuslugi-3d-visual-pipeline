## Purpose

Silver-Gold is a premium 3D system based on the metaphor of a stable technological platform with a limited value accent.

- Silver communicates infrastructure, neutrality, reliability, and technology.
- Gold communicates value, status, reward, and premium service.

Use it for premium products, increased yield, status levels, awards, selected badges, premium cards, and hero objects. Do not use it as the universal background language of the product.

## Primary invariants

### Material distribution

- Silver forms 70-85% of the visible object area and carries the main body and volume.
- Gold forms 15-30% and is limited to edges, symbols, rims, focal details, or logical points of attention.
- Gold expansion requires design review.
- Both metals use matte or satin surfaces.
- Reflections are minimal and controlled.

### Geometry

- low-poly or faceted geometric construction;
- clean edges and sharp folds;
- controlled symmetry when it reinforces stability;
- minimal decoration;
- precise metallic bevels without jewelry-like complexity.

### Lighting

- soft controlled rim light;
- dense clean shadows;
- subtle gradient-built volume;
- no uncontrolled bright hotspots;
- the light model must remain consistent across the whole object.

### Composition

- one dominant central object or one tightly controlled object group;
- dark or neutral background;
- controlled negative space;
- hierarchy remains readable at small size;
- the object must not become a full-frame metallic texture.

## Prompt vocabulary

Preferred markers:

- `3D render`;
- `low-poly geometric object`;
- `matte silver base` or `satin silver base`;
- `subtle gold accents`;
- `70-85 percent silver and 15-30 percent gold`;
- `dark or neutral background`;
- `minimal reflections`;
- `controlled rim light`;
- `clean edges and sharp folds`;
- `premium fintech aesthetic without jewelry styling`.

Do not rely on the name `Silver-Gold` alone. Compile the material and light parameters explicitly.

## Prohibitions

Reject or correct:

- mirror finish, chrome, glossy metal, or highly reflective surfaces;
- excessive reflections or bright specular hotspots;
- gold used as the dominant fill outside an explicitly approved flagship derivative;
- pseudo-jewelry decoration, gemstones, ornamental filigree, or luxury-kitsch cues;
- gradient kitsch or rainbow metallic effects;
- texture noise, scratches, dirt, or distressed metal;
- uncontrolled background reflections;
- multiple competing hero objects;
- use as a full-system background style.

## Pre-generation markers

A valid prompt plan must state:

- exact silver/gold target range;
- matte or satin finish;
- minimal reflections;
- controlled rim light;
- clean dense shadows;
- central hierarchy and negative space;
- explicit rejection of chrome, glossy, jewelry, and excessive gold.

## QA

The style gate fails when:

- silver is below 70% or gold exceeds 30% without an approved derivative;
- the surface reads as chrome or mirror metal;
- reflections dominate the form;
- gold loses its accent role;
- jewelry-like decoration replaces technological geometry;
- the light system is inconsistent;
- the style is used as a generic background rather than a controlled premium object.

Manual QA must record the estimated metal ratio, surface finish, reflection level, light consistency, negative-space quality, and approval requirement.
